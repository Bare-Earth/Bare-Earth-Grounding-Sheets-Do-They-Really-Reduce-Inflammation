## What Is Grounding, and Why Does It Matter?

![Alt text](https://offer.bareearthlife.com/images/landing-1/back-pain-original.webp)

The concept behind grounding—also called earthing—is rooted in basic bioelectrical science. The human body operates on electrical signals. Your nervous system, your heart, your cells: all of them rely on precise electrical activity to function. The earth's surface carries a natural supply of free electrons, and for most of human history, bare skin contact with the ground provided a continuous, passive exchange of that charge.

Modern life has largely severed that connection. Rubber-soled shoes, elevated buildings, synthetic flooring—we spend almost no time in direct contact with the ground. Some researchers believe this disconnection contributes to a chronic low-level buildup of oxidative stress and free radicals in the body, which plays a direct role in systemic inflammation.

When you ground, either by walking barefoot on grass or soil, or by using a conductive product while sleeping, electrons flow from the earth into your body. The proposed mechanism is that these electrons help neutralize positively charged free radicals—essentially acting as natural antioxidants at a cellular level. Bare Earth's product delivers this connection passively, through silver-threaded fabric, while you sleep.

Dr. Stephen Sinatra, an integrative cardiologist, described grounding as something that "shifts the body from disease mode into healing mode." Clint Ober, widely regarded as the founder of the modern grounding movement, has backed his position with over 20 peer-reviewed studies showing grounding's effects on inflammation, sleep quality, and nervous system regulation.

These aren't fringe claims. The science, while still developing, is credible and growing.
